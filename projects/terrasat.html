TERRASat: Terrain Electromagnetic Reconnaissance and Regional Analysis Satellite

TERRASat is a 12U CubeSat mission to Mars designed to measure localized magnetic fields using atmosphere-breathing electric propulsion (ABEP) and AI-driven crater detection. The satellite will collect high-resolution magnetometer data in low Mars orbit, providing insights into the planet’s crustal magnetism and potential past habitability.

My Role in TERRASat

As part of the mission design and orbital mechanics team, I focused on:

  • Designing the Earth-to-Mars transfer trajectory using MATLAB and STK.
  • Modeling the spiral escape trajectory from Earth’s 40,000 km parking orbit.
  • Optimizing delta-V calculations to determine the most efficient transfer window.
  • Developing the final low-altitude circular orbit around Mars.
  • Simulating autonomous course corrections for interplanetary navigation.

Mission Design & Trajectory

One of my key contributions was the creation of a pork chop plot to determine the optimal departure window for TERRASat’s transfer to Mars. This required solving Lambert’s problem in MATLAB to calculate the minimum delta-V required for the transfer.

Since TERRASat uses a low-thrust ion propulsion system, a standard Hohmann transfer was not feasible. Instead, I designed a continuous spiral trajectory to gradually expand the orbit until escape velocity was reached.

Challenges & Solutions

  • Ensuring orbital stability at low Mars altitudes while optimizing propellant usage.
  • Refining the ABEP system’s efficiency for long-duration propulsion in Mars' thin atmosphere.
  • Balancing the trade-offs between propulsion, science payload, and power constraints.

AI-Driven Crater Detection

While my primary work focused on trajectory design, I contributed to discussions on data processing techniques for TERRASat’s AI-driven crater detection. The onboard AI autonomously identifies scientifically relevant craters, reducing the amount of data transmitted back to Earth.

Key Results

  • Designed a complete Earth-to-Mars trajectory using MATLAB and STK.
  • Developed a spiral escape trajectory for low-thrust departure.
  • Modeled a stable low-altitude orbit around Mars for long-term data collection.
  • Performed delta-V calculations to determine the most efficient transfer window.

Next Steps

  • Validating ABEP efficiency for long-duration missions.
  • Integrating STK and MATLAB models for an end-to-end mission simulation.

TERRASat is an ambitious project demonstrating the potential of CubeSats in interplanetary exploration. My work in orbital mechanics and trajectory design plays a crucial role in ensuring the satellite can successfully navigate to Mars and maintain a stable orbit for scientific observations.
