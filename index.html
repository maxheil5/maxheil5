About Me

Hello! My name is Max Heil, and I’m a senior aerospace engineering student at The Ohio State University, set to graduate with my Bachelor of Science in 2025 and my Master of Science in 2026 (via BS/MS Program). My passion for aerospace engineering is driven by a love for experimentation, hands-on problem-solving, and pushing the boundaries of technology in aviation and space. I recently completed an internship at Collins Aerospace in avionics advanced manufacturing, where I worked on solving real-world challenges in aerospace technology. Now, I’m deeply immersed in experimental research, including projects on quadcopter dynamics and the effects of quantization using innovative dither techniques. I’m also exploring advanced space propulsion systems through my work on the TERRASat project—a CubeSat mission to Mars that utilizes atmosphere-breathing electric propulsion to measure localized magnetic fields. This project has been a unique opportunity to apply my skills in mission design, MATLAB simulations, and trajectory modeling to real-world space exploration. My interests extend to advanced space propulsion, where I’m eager to contribute to the development of new technologies that can shape the future of space exploration. In my free time, I take to the skies with my private pilot’s license, build custom drones, and experiment with 3D printing. I recently launched a small business creating 3D-printed products, combining technical expertise with entrepreneurial ambition. Whether it's testing new ideas in the lab, building complex systems, or exploring the skies, I’m driven by the challenge of turning innovative concepts into tangible results. Through my work, I aim to make a significant impact on aerospace technology and inspire others to pursue their own high-flying ambitions. Feel free to explore my portfolio and reach out—I’d love to connect!

Experience

Laboratory for Autonomy in Data-Driven and Complex Systems (LADDCS)

Space Systems Graduate Researcher

I explore intent estimation theory within the Hill frame by analyzing and categorizing unknown spacecraft maneuvers to identify best-fit intent models, utilizing probabilistic methods and game-theory approaches. Building on this foundation, I am developing an expanded set of basis maneuvers to improve the accuracy of intent predictions. To enhance maneuver detection, I incorporate Adaptive Monte Carlo (AMC) methods for Bayesian inference, effectively reducing uncertainties. Additionally, I investigate kill chain methodologies for acquiring, tracking, and ensuring asset protection in space as part of my work with the SDA TAP Lab.

Systems, Optimization, and Autonomous Robotics Laboratory (SOAR)

Aerospace Controls Undergraduate Researcher

I develop and validate data-driven control systems for UAVs using extended dynamic mode decomposition (EDMD) and model predictive control (MPC) techniques in MATLAB and ROS. My work includes investigating the impact of dither quantization on UAV control performance in resource-limited environments. To bridge theory and application, I conduct SITL and HITL testing using a PX4-Starling Autonomy drone, enabling the analysis of real-world UAV dynamics. Additionally, I design robust methods for experimental testing, including the implementation of control schemes, flight tests, and performance benchmarking.

Collins Aerospace

Avionics Advanced Manufacturing Engineering Intern

I developed robust simulations in Visual Components to evaluate bottlenecks in automated manufacturing modules, presenting detailed renderings and analyses to leadership, which secured $3 million in funding for the automation project. Leveraging additive manufacturing, I utilized Markforged Metal X and Prusa printers to design and implement 3D-printed components, optimizing strength-to-weight ratios and reducing production costs by over $24,000. Additionally, I led the end-to-end validation of an RFID system to enhance WIP tracking and traceability, ensuring seamless integration into existing workflows. My contributions extended to NPI testing, where I assessed the impact of various environmental conditions on avionics systems. Further, I operated Boeing 737 MAX simulators, gaining hands-on experience in test bed setup and troubleshooting methodologies critical to avionics development.
